The group you’re thinking of is the Ikhwan al-Safa (إخوان الصفا), usually translated as the Brethren of Purity or Brethren of Sincerity.

They were an intellectual brotherhood active in the 10th century, most likely in Basra (in present-day Iraq) under the Abbasid Caliphate. Their project was encyclopedic and synthetic: they sought to reconcile Greek philosophy—especially Pythagorean and Neoplatonic traditions—with Islamic theology.

Their major work is the Rasa’il Ikhwan al-Safa’ (“Epistles of the Brethren of Purity”), a large corpus of treatises covering:

• Arithmetic and number theory (strongly Pythagorean)
• Geometry and harmonics (music as mathematical proportion)
• Astronomy and cosmology
• Logic and natural philosophy
• Psychology and the hierarchy of souls
• Political philosophy and ethics
• Esoteric theology

The Pythagorean dimension is explicit. They treated number as ontologically primary, described cosmic harmony in mathematical terms, and developed a graded emanationist cosmology influenced by Neoplatonism. The universe unfolds from the One through Intellect and Soul in a structured, numerical order.

They also framed themselves as a secretive ethical fraternity: philosophical purification was both intellectual and moral. Their aim was the refinement of the soul through knowledge, discipline, and fraternity.

If you're mapping intellectual genealogies for something like your esoteric knowledge system, they sit at a crucial junction between:

Greek mathematical mysticism (Pythagoreanism)
Late antique Neoplatonism
Islamic theology
Later Latin scholastic transmission

They are one of the clearest examples of a genuinely “Islamicate Pythagorean” brotherhood.
